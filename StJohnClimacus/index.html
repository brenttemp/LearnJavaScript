Saint John Climacus

Author of the Ladder of the Divine Ascent
Ikon of Saint John Climacus, also called St. John of the Ladder

    Here is a list of what is known about his life:

  • c. 525- Born in Syria
  • c. 541- At around the age of 16, St. John became a novice at what is now known as St. Catherine's Monastery at Mount Sinai
  • c. 541-Unkown- St. John was a student of the elder monk Martyrius, from whom he learned about the spiritual life
  • After the death of Maryrius, St. John lived in isolation at the foot of Mount Sinai for twenty years in order to practice greater asceticism
  • c. 600- He became the Igumen or abbot of the monks of Sinai
  • Around this time, he wrote the Κλῖμαξ or Ladder of Divine Ascent

      The Ladder of Divine Ascent includes the following 30 Steps:

    1. On Renunciation of the World or On Asceticism
    2. On Detachment
    3. On Exile or Pilgrimage - Concerning dreams that beginners have
    4. On Obedience
    5. On True Repentance
    6. On Rememberance of Death
    7. On Joy-making Mourning
    8. On Freedom from Anger and on Meekness
    9. On Remembrance of Wrongs
    10. On Slander or Calumny
    11. On Talkativeness and Silence
    12. On Lying
    13. On Desponency
    14. On That Clamorous Mistress, the Stomach
    15. On Incorruptible Purity and Chastity, to which the Corruptible Attain by Toil and Sweat
    16. On Love of Money, or Avarice
    17. On Non-possessiveness
    18. On Insensibility
    19. On Sleep, Prayer, and Psalmody with the Brotherhood
    20. On Bodily Vigil and how to use it to Attain Spiritual Vigil
    21. On Unmanly and Puerile Cowardice
    22. On the Many Forms of Vainglory
    23. On Mad Pride and on Unclean Blasphemous Thoughts
    24. On Meekness, Simplicity, and Guilelessness
    25. On Sublime Humility
    26. On Discernment of Thoughts, Passions and Virtues
    27. On Holy Stillness of Body and Soul- different aspects of stillness and how to distinguish them
    28. On Holy and Blessed Prayer- the attitude of mind and body in prayer
    29. Concerning Heaven on Earth- the resurrection of the soul before general resurrection
    30. Concerning the Linking Together of the Supreme Trinity among the Virtues- a brief exhortation summarizing the book
  • c. 649- St. John died at Mount Sinai
  • St. John's feast day is March 30th, and is commemorated in the Eastern Orthodox Church on the Fourth Sunday of Great Lent

"Do not be surprised that you fall every day; do not give up, but stand your ground courageously. And assuredly, the angel who guards you will honor your patience."

-- St. John of the Ladder

    Troparion

  • By a flood of tears you made the desert fertile
  • And by your longing for God you brought forth fruits in abundance.
  • By the radiance of miracles you illumniated the whole universe.
  • O our holy Father John Climacus, pray to Christ our God to save our souls.

    • Kontakion

  • You offered us your teachings as fruits of everlasting freshness,
  • To sweeten the hearts fo those who receive them with attention
  • O blessed and wise John, they are the rungs fo a ladder,
  • Leading the souls of those who honor you from earth to Eternal Glory in Heaven
